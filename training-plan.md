## Read this first — how to use the plan

A few things to keep in mind, since this plan covers a wide age range and a short, race-heavy stretch:

- **Scale by age and experience.** A 10-year-old and a 17-year-old should not do the same volume. Younger or newer runners do the **low end** of every rep range and stop a rep or two early if form falls apart; older, experienced runners can do the **high end**. When in doubt at this age, *less is better* — consistency and staying healthy beat any single hard workout.
- **The races are most of the training stimulus.** With a meet almost every Wednesday, the in-person sessions are kept moderate. The goal Mon/Tue is to *sharpen and support* racing, not to bury the kids.
- **These are coaching judgment calls, not guarantees.** I can't see each athlete's fitness, history, or how they respond. Treat paces and rep counts as starting points and adjust to what you actually see. Stop or back off for pain (especially shin, knee, hip, or heel — common in growing kids), unusual fatigue, or bad heat. This is a training template, not medical advice.
- **Summer heat (Michigan):** hydrate before/after, move sessions earlier into shade if needed, and slow the easy/long runs in heat — effort matters more than the clock on those days.
- **You'll need a recent goal pace per athlete** (see next section). Without a recent race or time trial, the percentages can't be set accurately.

## Setting "percentage of max" speed targets

You asked for speed targets as a percentage anchored to **goal race pace**. Here's exactly how that works, because the math is easy to get backwards.

**Define 100% = the athlete's goal race-pace *speed*.** Higher % = **faster** than race pace; lower % = **slower/easier**.

Each athlete anchors to **their own primary event's** goal pace — an 800 specialist uses goal 800 pace, a 3200 specialist uses goal 3200 pace. So "100%" is a different clock time for different kids, which is correct.

**Converting a % to a lap split:**

> lap time at X% = (goal-pace lap time) ÷ (X ÷ 100)

*Worked example — a runner with a 6:00 goal 1600 (= 90 sec per 400 m lap):*
- 105% → 90 ÷ 1.05 = **~86 sec/lap** (faster than race pace)
- 100% → **90 sec/lap** (race pace)
- 90% → 90 ÷ 0.90 = **100 sec/lap**
- 75% → 90 ÷ 0.75 = **120 sec/lap** (easy)

### Intensity zones (starting guide)

| Zone | % of goal pace | Feels like | Used for |
|---|---|---|---|
| Recovery / easy | 65–75% | Conversational, relaxed | Recovery & easy days |
| Aerobic / steady | 78–85% | Comfortable, "all day" | Easy & long runs |
| Tempo / threshold | 86–92% | "Comfortably hard," controlled | Tempo / cruise reps |
| Race pace | ~98–102% | Race effort for the event | Race-pace sharpening |
| VO₂ / speed-endurance | 103–112% | Hard, fast, controlled form | Interval reps |
| Pure speed (short) | 113%+ | Near-max, short, full rest | Strides / short hills |

> **Honest caveat:** Percentages are most precise for **race-pace and faster** work. For **easy, long, and tempo** running, anchoring to race pace is fuzzier — coach those by **effort/breathing** (conversational = easy; comfortably hard = tempo) and use the % only as a rough ceiling so kids don't run easy days too fast (a very common mistake).

**6:00–6:15 — Group warm-up & drills (everyone together)**
- 5 min easy jog
- Dynamic drills: leg swings, walking lunges, A-skips, B-skips, high knees, butt kicks, carioca

**7:15 — Group stretch & announcements (everyone together)**

## Twice-a-week strength & mobility (home, ~15 min)

Bodyweight only, great for young runners and injury prevention. Suggest **2 non-consecutive days** (e.g., Fri + Sun).
- Bodyweight squats — 2 × 10–15
- Walking lunges — 2 × 8–10 per leg
- Glute bridges — 2 × 12–15
- Plank — 2 × 20–40 sec
- Side plank — 2 × 15–30 sec each side
- Calf raises — 2 × 15–20
- Single-leg balance — 30 sec each side

Keep it light and consistent; this is durability work, not a lifting program.

## Event-focus summary (how 800 vs 1600 vs 3200 differ here)

- **800 focus:** fewer, faster, shorter reps (more speed-endurance, >100% work); slightly less aerobic/long-run volume.
- **1600 focus:** the balanced middle — reps right around race pace; moderate aerobic volume.
- **3200 / 5k focus:** longer reps at slightly lower %, the longest long runs, more total aerobic volume.

Most of practice is shared — same session type, just different rep counts and % targets per the columns above.
