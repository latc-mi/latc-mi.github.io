How to Register

Getting your athlete on the LATC team is easy — follow these simple steps.

  1. Check Eligibility

    LATC is open to all athletes in grades K–12 who reside in the greater Lansing area. Athletes should be in good academic standing and able to participate in regular practices.

  2. Complete the Registration Form

    Fill out the online registration form with your athlete's information, emergency contacts, and event preferences. You'll also need to provide a copy of your athlete's birth certificate and current school ID.

    Online Registration Form

  3. Submit the Registration Fee

    The registration fee covers all practices, uniform, coaching, and meet entry fees for the season. Payment can be made online via credit/debit card or check made out to "Lansing Area Track Club."

    Registration Fees
    • Single Athlete: $150
    • Family Rate (2+ athletes): $120 per athlete
    • Financial assistance available — contact us

  4. Return Medical & Waiver Forms

    Download and complete the medical history form and liability waiver. Return signed forms to the registration table at the first practice or email a scanned copy to {{ site.email }}.

    Download Forms (PDF)

  5. Show Up & Run!

    Attend the first practice on {{ site.data.season.season_start }} at {{ site.data.season.practice_location }}. Wear comfortable athletic clothing and bring water. Your uniform will be distributed at the first or second practice.

Frequently Asked Questions

My child has never done track before. Is LATC right for them?
Absolutely! LATC welcomes athletes of all experience levels, from complete beginners to experienced competitors. Our coaches specialize in developing young athletes and will work with your child at their current level. Many of our most successful athletes started with no prior experience.
What events can my child compete in?
Athletes can compete in sprints (100m, 200m, 400m), middle distance (800m, 1600m), long distance (3200m), relays (4×100m, 4×400m), and field events including long jump, high jump, triple jump, and shot put. Discus is available for high school athletes. Coaches will help identify the best events based on your athlete's strengths and interests.
What if we can't afford the registration fee?
We believe every athlete deserves the opportunity to compete. LATC offers a limited number of need-based scholarships and payment plans. Please contact us at {{ site.email }} to discuss options — all inquiries are kept confidential.
Does my child need spikes or special equipment?
Regular athletic shoes (sneakers) are perfectly fine, especially for younger or beginner athletes. As athletes advance, track spikes can improve performance, particularly for sprints and jumping events. Coaches can advise on whether spikes make sense for your child's events and level. No other special equipment is required beyond the team uniform.
Can my child miss practices or meets?
We understand that life happens! We ask athletes to make every effort to attend practices and scheduled meets. If your child needs to miss a session, please notify the coach in advance. Consistent attendance is important for athletic development and team cohesion, but we are family-friendly and accommodating.
How can parents get involved?
Parents are the heart of our club! Volunteer opportunities include meet day setup/cleanup, timing and official roles, providing snacks, and helping with fundraising. Parent volunteers make our home invitational possible. Contact us at {{ site.email }} to get involved.
Is there a refund policy?
Full refunds are available before the season begins (prior to {{ site.data.season.season_start }}). After the season starts, a 50% refund is available within the first two weeks. No refunds after the first two weeks of the season, as uniform orders and meet entries are already placed.
